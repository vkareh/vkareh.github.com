Module development and other things

September 11, 2010

After almost 9 months working for Switchback, I finally sat down to publish another module on the Drupal website.

During my time there, I've developed countless modules, and (hopefully) helped improve a lot more, but the web development world can be busy enough that we don't get time to do a lot of contrib work.

In retrospect, I do contribute to Drupal, as shown in my track, but it's usually relegated to other people's modules. It is very satisfying to help improve existing projects that many websites use, all in a community-driven way. Still, there is something to be said about publishing and maintaining your own module. It gives you a sense of ownership and permanency in an environment where you sometimes have to move from one website to another before getting to fully understand them.

That said, last night I sat down to publish the Clear saved password field module. In less that 12 hours, a couple of people opened up issues for new feature requests, and one of them was even for inclusion into Core. Nice.

This can only reaffirm my commitment to Drupal and open source in general. Now I just need to actually sit down and do it!
